From De Anza to Cal Poly

I'm excited to share my journey of transferring from De Anza College to the Computer Science program at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo (Cal Poly SLO). This experience has been instrumental in my pursuit of a software engineering career, and I hope my insights can be helpful to others considering a similar path.

Building a Strong Foundation at De Anza

De Anza provided a rigorous computer science curriculum that proved essential for my development. Courses in C++, Java, Data Structures, and Discrete Mathematics established a solid technical base and honed my problem-solving abilities.

Balancing Passions: Coaching and Coding

While academics were a priority, I also remained active in my passion for sports by serving as a Track and Field Assistant Coach. Balancing coaching responsibilities with a demanding academic schedule enhanced my time management and leadership skills. Additionally, I gained practical experience as a Warehouse Logistics Specialist, where I leveraged my programming skills to automate inventory management tasks using Python.

Overcoming Challenges, Achieving Success

My academic journey was not without its challenges. Calculus and physics presented significant hurdles, prompting a brief pause in my studies. However, this setback only strengthened my resolve. I returned with renewed focus, successfully completing advanced calculus and consistently earning a place on the Dean's List.

Gaining Practical Experience

My time at De Anza included valuable professional experiences. As a Junior Quality Assurance Analyst at Milestone Technologies and a Freelance Data Analyst, I applied my skills in Python, SQL, and data visualization tools. I gained experience leading teams in Agile environments, automating testing processes, and developing data-driven solutions. These roles enriched my technical expertise and emphasized the importance of collaboration and continuous learning in the workplace.

Reaching for Cal Poly SLO

With a strong academic record and diverse professional experiences, I applied to the highly competitive Computer Science program at Cal Poly SLO. I was thrilled to be accepted and eager to embrace the university's renowned "Learn by Doing" philosophy.

Embracing Cal Poly's "Learn by Doing" Philosophy

Cal Poly's emphasis on "Learn by Doing" aligns perfectly with my learning style and career aspirations. Engaging in complex projects, such as developing scalable backend systems and cross-platform applications, has further strengthened my technical skills and prepared me for the demands of the software engineering industry.

Looking Ahead

My journey from De Anza to Cal Poly SLO has equipped me with a unique combination of academic knowledge and practical experience. I am excited to continue my studies, pursue internships, and ultimately contribute to the field of software engineering as a skilled and innovative professional.
